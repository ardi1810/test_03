The Untold Story Of Steve Jobs: A Review Of The 2015 Netflix Film

The Film's Premise

The 2015 Netflix film, "Steve Jobs," directed by Danny Boyle, tells the story of the Apple co-founder's life in three acts, each taking place backstage before one of his iconic product launches. The film stars Michael Fassbender as Steve Jobs, Kate Winslet as his trusted confidant, Joanna Hoffman, and Seth Rogen as Steve Wozniak, Jobs' partner in founding Apple.

The Film's Reception

The film was met with mixed reviews upon its release, with some praising the performances of the actors and the innovative structure of the story, while others criticized it for taking too many liberties with the facts of Jobs' life.

Michael Fassbender's Performance

One of the most notable aspects of the film is Michael Fassbender's portrayal of Steve Jobs. Fassbender manages to capture the essence of Jobs' charisma, ambition, and drive, while also conveying his flaws and vulnerabilities. His performance is widely regarded as one of the best of his career.

The Real Steve Jobs

Of course, it's important to remember that the film is a work of fiction and doesn't necessarily reflect the real Steve Jobs. However, it does offer an interesting interpretation of his life and legacy, and provides a valuable insight into the mind of one of the most influential figures in tech history.

The Film's Structure

The film's structure is also worth noting. As mentioned before, it is divided into three acts, each taking place backstage before one of Jobs' product launches. This allows the film to focus on the relationships between Jobs and the people in his life, rather than on the products themselves.

A Unique Approach

This unique approach to storytelling sets "Steve Jobs" apart from other biopics, and allows the film to delve deeper into Jobs' personality and motivations. It also gives the audience a sense of the pressure and chaos that Jobs must have experienced in the lead-up to each product launch.

The Film's Soundtrack

The film's soundtrack, composed by Daniel Pemberton, is another standout element of the film. Pemberton's score is a mix of electronic and orchestral music, which perfectly captures the energy and excitement of Jobs' product launches.

The Impact of the Film

While the film may not have been a commercial success, it has had a lasting impact on the way that people view Steve Jobs and his legacy. It has sparked renewed interest in his life and work, and has encouraged many to take a closer look at the man behind the myth.

Conclusion

"Steve Jobs" is a must-see film for anyone interested in the history of technology or the life and legacy of Steve Jobs. While it may not be entirely accurate, it provides a unique and compelling interpretation of Jobs' life, and features some truly outstanding performances from its cast.

Whether you're a fan of Apple or simply interested in the life of a tech icon, "Steve Jobs" is well worth a watch.
