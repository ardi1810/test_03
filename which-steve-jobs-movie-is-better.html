The Steve Jobs Filmography

Steve Jobs is a name synonymous with innovation, technology, and of course, Apple. It's no surprise that Hollywood has taken an interest in his life, and over the years, several movies have been made about the tech icon. In this article, we'll take a look at the two most popular Steve Jobs movies and determine which one is better.

Steve Jobs (2015)

The first movie on our list is "Steve Jobs," directed by Danny Boyle and starring Michael Fassbender as the titular character. The film is a biographical drama that follows Jobs through three product launches, offering a glimpse into his personal and professional life. The movie is divided into three acts, each of which takes place backstage before the launch of a new product. The first act is set in 1984, with the launch of the Macintosh computer. The second act takes place in 1988, during the launch of the NeXT computer. Finally, the third act is set in 1998, during the unveiling of the iMac.

Jobs (2013)

The second movie on our list is "Jobs," directed by Joshua Michael Stern and starring Ashton Kutcher as Steve Jobs. The film is a biographical drama that covers Jobs' life from 1974 to 2000, focusing on his early years, the founding of Apple, and his eventual departure from the company. Unlike "Steve Jobs," which focuses on three product launches, "Jobs" covers a broader timeline, offering a more comprehensive look at Jobs' life and career. The movie also features a larger ensemble cast, including Josh Gad as Steve Wozniak and Dermot Mulroney as Mike Markkula.

Which Movie is Better?

Now that we've covered both movies, it's time to determine which one is better. In terms of critical reception, "Steve Jobs" is the clear winner. The movie was praised for its performances, writing, and direction, earning an 85% approval rating on Rotten Tomatoes. "Jobs," on the other hand, received mixed reviews, with a 27% approval rating on Rotten Tomatoes. While "Steve Jobs" may be the better-reviewed movie, it's important to note that both films offer a different perspective on Jobs' life and career. "Steve Jobs" focuses on a specific period and offers a more nuanced look at the man behind the Apple brand. "Jobs," on the other hand, covers a broader timeline and offers a more traditional biopic approach. Ultimately, the choice between the two movies comes down to personal preference. If you're looking for a more focused and intense look at Jobs' life, "Steve Jobs" is the way to go. If you're interested in a more comprehensive overview of his career, "Jobs" may be the better choice.

Conclusion

In conclusion, both "Steve Jobs" and "Jobs" offer unique perspectives on the life and career of Steve Jobs. While "Steve Jobs" may be the better-reviewed movie, both films are worth watching for fans of the tech icon. So, which Steve Jobs movie is better? The answer is subjective, and it ultimately comes down to what you're looking for in a movie.
